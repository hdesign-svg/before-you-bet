# Primary User Scenario — Before You Bet

*One specific person. One specific moment. This is the story the entire product is designed around.*

---

## The Person

**Susan** is in her late 50s. She watches football every Sunday, mostly to stay connected to her family and the people around her. A couple years ago, sports betting became legal in her state, and the apps made it easy to get started. Now she places $10–$20 bets a few times a week during the NFL season — small amounts, but they add up.

She's not a gambler. She's a participant. Betting makes the games more fun and gives her something to talk about. But she doesn't understand spreads, over/unders, or how to read matchup data. She relies on gut feelings, what she heard on TV, and texts to her son asking "What do you think about the Chiefs?"

## The Moment

It's Sunday morning, 10:45 AM. Susan is at brunch with her son. She just realized she hasn't placed her bets or set her fantasy lineup. Games start in two hours.

She pulls out her phone and opens DraftKings. The screen is a wall of numbers — spreads, parlays, player props. She doesn't know what half of it means. She turns to her son: "Should I take the Chiefs? What about the over?"

Her son does his best. He knows the Chiefs' offensive line has been strong and their running back is healthy, but the other team has good linebackers. He's not confident. He pulls up ESPN — too many articles, too much data. They try ChatGPT — it gives a decent but generic answer that she'll forget by next week.

The clock is ticking. Brunch is no longer relaxing. What started as a casual morning has turned into a stressful scramble.

She makes her picks based on a mix of gut feeling, her son's half-confident advice, and whatever she could piece together in 15 minutes. She loses $40 that week.

## What She's Feeling

- **Anxiety** — the clock is ticking, games are about to start
- **Overwhelm** — too many numbers, too much jargon, too many options
- **Dependence** — she can't figure this out on her own, so she leans on her son
- **Guilt** — she knows she's ruining the vibe at brunch
- **FOMO** — she doesn't want to miss placing her bets, everyone else is doing it
- **Frustration** — she's been doing this for months and still doesn't feel any smarter about it

## What She Currently Does

1. **Opens her sportsbook app** → sees lines and parlays she doesn't understand
2. **Texts her son** → gets half-confident advice that makes them both uncomfortable
3. **Checks ESPN or Google** → finds long articles and stat tables that don't help in the moment
4. **Tries ChatGPT (occasionally)** → gets decent analysis but doesn't remember to use it, and the output is generic
5. **Makes a gut-feeling pick** → loses money more often than not
6. **Repeats next week** → no learning curve, no improvement

## What She Needs (But Can't Articulate)

She'd say: *"Just tell me who to bet on."*

What she actually needs:
- **Context** — what's the story of this game, in plain English?
- **Translation** — what do these betting lines actually mean?
- **Focus** — which 2-3 players should I be paying attention to, and why?
- **Confidence** — one clear takeaway that makes her feel informed, not anxious

She doesn't need more data. She doesn't need picks. She needs to *understand enough to make her own decision* — and feel good about it either way.

## The Before You Bet Experience

**Saturday evening, 8 PM.** Susan gets a push notification (future state) or remembers to open Before You Bet. She sees:

**Homepage — Week 12**
Three game cards. Clean, minimal. Each one shows the teams, the spread in plain English, and a one-line insight. No jargon. No clutter. She taps into Chiefs @ Bills because that's the game she's been thinking about.

**Game Detail — Chiefs @ Bills**

*The Story:* Two sentences that catch her up. The Chiefs are on a hot streak. The Bills are tough at home. This is a big game with playoff implications. She now has context she didn't have before.

*What the Odds Really Say:* Three bullet points. "Vegas thinks Buffalo wins by about a field goal." "They expect a lot of scoring." "The market sees this as a close game." She finally understands what -3.5 means — and it took 10 seconds.

*Key Players That Matter:* Patrick Mahomes and Josh Allen, each with four simple fields. She learns Mahomes is playing at an elite level but the Bills' secondary has been giving up big plays. She understands *why* this matters for her bet.

*The Takeaway:* One sentence. "This game is a coin flip, but if you're betting, the Bills at home have earned the slight edge."

**The result:** Susan closes the app. She doesn't text her son. She doesn't open ChatGPT. She goes to DraftKings and places her bet — not with anxiety, but with understanding. She might still lose. But she made an informed decision, and that feels fundamentally different.

Total time: **under 60 seconds.**
