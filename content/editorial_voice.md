# Editorial Voice Guide — Before You Bet

*How we sound. Every word should feel like a smart friend who watches a lot of football explaining things over a beer.*

---

## Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Clear | Simple sentences, no jargon | Dumbed down or condescending |
| Confident | We have a point of view | Cocky or guaranteed |
| Simple | Easy to scan and understand | Shallow or lacking depth |
| Human | Conversational, warm | Slangy or unprofessional |

---

## Examples

### Instead of this:
> "Kansas City is favored by 7.5 points with an over/under of 48.5, reflecting market sentiment toward a high-scoring affair with the Chiefs' offense expected to dominate."

### Write this:
> "Vegas thinks the Chiefs win by about a touchdown, and that this game will have a lot of scoring. Translation: they expect KC's offense to roll."

---

### Instead of this:
> "Pacheco is questionable with a knee injury, which may impact his snap count and limit his rushing attempts."

### Write this:
> "Pacheco might play, but he's banged up. Even if he suits up, don't expect a full workload. His backup could see real action."

---

## Guardrails
- Never say "guaranteed" or "lock"
- Never use "bet the farm" or gambling-encouraging language
- Always translate odds/lines when they appear
- Use "we" and "you" — never "the user" or "one"
