# Problem Definition — What's Broken

---

## The Problem

Beginner bettors are making uninformed, emotionally-driven decisions because the tools available to them are designed for experts, and the platforms they bet on are designed to encourage action — not understanding.

## Why It Exists

The incentive structures are misaligned:

- **Sportsbooks profit from volume, not accuracy.** Their apps are optimized to make placing a bet frictionless. Understanding the bet? That's your problem.
- **Sports media is built for the already-fluent.** ESPN, PFF, and betting content sites assume you know what a spread is, what ATS means, and how to read a stat line. If you don't, you bounce.
- **AI tools require initiative.** ChatGPT can give decent analysis if you know what to ask — but most people don't, and it's not a habit. It's a one-off scramble, not a system.
- **There's no "clarity layer" in the market.** Plenty of products help you bet. Plenty help you analyze. Nothing is designed to help you *understand* — in plain English, at a glance, before you make a decision.

## Who Feels It Most

The person we're calling **Susan**: someone who watches football, enjoys the social aspect of betting, and participates weekly — but doesn't speak the language of odds, lines, and spreads. She's not reckless. She's under-informed. And every product available to her either assumes expertise she doesn't have or pushes her toward action before understanding.

She's the person making $10-$20 bets multiple times a week based on gut feelings, friend recommendations, or whatever she can piece together in the 20 minutes before kickoff.

## What Happens If Nothing Changes

- **Financial:** Small, consistent losses compound. $20/week over a 5-month NFL season is $400+ lost — not catastrophic, but meaningful. Especially as it becomes a habit that extends to other sports.
- **Emotional:** The cycle of anxiety → scramble → loss → guilt repeats weekly. Betting stops being fun and becomes stressful.
- **Relational:** People like Susan lean on family and friends for answers. Those people (sons, partners, coworkers) feel the weight of that responsibility — and the guilt when the advice doesn't land.
- **Behavioral:** Without understanding, there's no learning curve. Susan doesn't get better at betting over time. She just keeps guessing.

## What Would "Solved" Look Like

Susan opens Before You Bet on Saturday evening or Sunday morning. In under 60 seconds, she understands:
- What's actually happening in the games she cares about
- What the betting lines mean in plain English
- Which players matter and why
- Whether a bet feels smart or risky — and why

She doesn't need to text her son. She doesn't need to open ChatGPT. She doesn't need to read a 1,500-word article. She just *understands* — and makes her decision with confidence instead of anxiety.

The feeling: *"Oh, now I get it. I'm good."*
